By George S.
November 7, 2023

In the world of modern cinema, few storytellers have made as significant an impact as Taylor Sheridan. Known for his exceptional ability to craft gripping narratives that delve into the complexities of the American frontier, Sheridan has left an indelible mark on the film and television industry. From his early days as an actor to his emergence as a formidable writer and director, Sheridan's genius storytelling has consistently captivated audiences and critics alike. 

The Western Revivalist

Taylor Sheridan's storytelling prowess shines most brightly when he explores the Western genre. Unlike the Westerns of the past, which often followed the clichéd themes of heroism and villainy, Sheridan's narratives are deeply rooted in realism and moral ambiguity. His ability to subvert traditional tropes and breathe new life into the genre has earned him a reputation as a Western revivalist.

In "Hell or High Water" (2016), Sheridan created a modern-day Western that grapples with economic hardship, family bonds, and a world where the lines between right and wrong blur. The film's protagonists, played by Chris Pine and Ben Foster, are driven by a sense of desperation, leading them to commit a series of bank robberies. While they are technically criminals, the audience sympathizes with their plight, highlighting Sheridan's knack for making the audience question their own moral compass.

Similarly, in "Wind River" (2017), Sheridan delves into the bleak and unforgiving landscapes of the American wilderness. The film's exploration of justice, grief, and redemption showcases the darker aspects of the Western genre, while also serving as a profound commentary on the plight of Native Americans. Through his storytelling, Sheridan masterfully crafts an emotionally resonant narrative that brings to light the harsh realities of life in the American frontier.



Complex Characters and Nuanced Themes

One of Taylor Sheridan's signature strengths is his ability to create complex and multifaceted characters. His characters are never one-dimensional; they are shaped by their experiences, their environment, and their moral dilemmas. This character development elevates his storytelling to a whole new level, making his narratives profoundly relatable and thought-provoking.



In "Sicario" (2015), Sheridan introduced audiences to the enigmatic character of Alejandro Gillick, portrayed by Benicio Del Toro. A mysterious hitman with a tragic past, Gillick is both ruthless and sympathetic. His character personifies the moral ambiguity that runs throughout the film, leaving the audience torn between rooting for justice and questioning the means to achieve it.

Furthermore, Sheridan often explores themes that reflect the current sociopolitical landscape. In "Yellowstone," a television series that he created, he examines the tension between ranchers, land developers, and Native American tribes. This series delves into issues of land rights, family loyalty, and the exploitation of natural resources. By weaving these themes into his storytelling, Sheridan sparks important conversations about contemporary American society.

Narrative Techniques

Sheridan's storytelling brilliance also shines through his use of narrative techniques. He employs a non-linear storytelling approach that keeps audiences engaged and invested in his narratives. His mastery of tension-building and suspense keeps viewers on the edge of their seats, unable to predict the direction of the story. Sheridan also excels in creating atmospheric settings, from the barren landscapes of West Texas to the snow-covered wilderness of Wyoming, which become integral elements of his narratives.

Everything feels so real!

"Mayor of Kingstown" is a crime drama television series created by Taylor Sheridan and Hugh Dillon. Premiered in November 2021, the show is part of Taylor Sheridan's growing portfolio of work that includes hit series like "Yellowstone" and critically acclaimed films such as "Sicario" and "Wind River." Set in the fictional town of Kingstown, Michigan, the series explores the complex and often brutal world of incarceration, criminal justice, and family dynamics.

The story revolves around the McLusky family, who hold significant power and influence in Kingstown. The McLuskys are uniquely positioned at the intersection of politics, the police force, and the prison system. As the town's self-appointed mayor, Mike McLusky (played by Jeremy Renner) plays a central role in maintaining order and control within the prison system. The show delves into the intricate relationships within the McLusky family and the challenges they face in a town where crime and justice are inextricably linked.

"Mayor of Kingstown" is notable for its gritty and realistic portrayal of the criminal justice system, including the challenges faced by both inmates and those who oversee them. The series touches on themes of corruption, power struggles, and the blurred lines between right and wrong. It also explores the personal and professional dilemmas of the characters as they navigate this treacherous landscape.

Taylor Sheridan's involvement in the series brings his signature storytelling style to the forefront. As in his other works, he creates complex, morally ambiguous characters and situations that keep viewers engaged. The show's intense and often dark narrative, combined with its rich character development, has garnered positive reviews and has solidified Taylor Sheridan's reputation as a master storyteller in the realm of crime dramas.

"Mayor of Kingstown" is part of the growing trend of prestige television that explores the intricacies of the criminal justice system and its impact on individuals and communities. With its compelling storytelling, strong performances, and thought-provoking themes, the series has captured the attention of audiences and promises to be a significant addition to Taylor Sheridan's body of work.

Before you go

In order to understand the impact of Sheridan's writing you have to dig deep into the mines of human conscience. The characters always feel so alive and fragile, there are no protagonists really, only a set of characters doomed to star in an elaborate plan of reality that could not be visualized in the craziest fiction plot. The delivery of even the most minor characters is flawless and I am sure it was meticulously done in that way in order to commemorate that this is the real world. One of the most magical experiences when watching a Sheridan “story” is the fact that after a while you forget you’re watching your favorite actor doing his job. I forgot Jeremy Renner was in the Avengers after half an hour of watching him in Mayor of Kingstown. Renner was great as Gary Webb in “Kill the Messenger” but Mike McLusky feels like a real person who borrowed Renner’s posture and voice. This is the astonishing reality you have to confront when watching Sheridan’s work, the actors feel real, gritty, and vulnerable and every confrontation seems to be happening in front of your eyes. Every shot and every punch feel so real you actually feel your stomach clenching because of the realization that this is happening somewhere to someone, and Sheridan makes it feel like this is the perfect visualization of something horrible yet so artistic.
